Positive Disruption Data Explorer

The Data Explorer is a web-based tool that allows for easy navigation through the Positive Disruption model results to examine how adoption of low- and no-carbon technologies and practices in the energy, agriculture, and land-use sectors can be expected to grow over the next 30 years, and the effect they will have on the process of reversing climate change.

Positive Disruption

The past several decades have seen extensive analysis of climate change science, and there is now an imminent need and remarkable opportunity to focus on climate solutions. While much of the existing climate science portrays the 1.5Â°C target as challenging given business-as-usual emissions projected from the energy and land use sectors, there is strong evidence to suggest that the future will not be business as usual. Rather, energy and land use will be disrupted by fast-emerging low- and negative-carbon technologies and practices. Governments, industries, and communities would benefit from decarbonization pathways that recognize this potential for Positive Disruption speed of scaling.
